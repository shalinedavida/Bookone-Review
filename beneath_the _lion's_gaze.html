ISSUES RAISED

FAMILY

In the book, Beneath the Lion's gaze, family is a key issue portrayed in the book.
The book is mainly set up in Hailu’s householdwhereby he has two sons, Yonas
and Dawait. Yonas marries Sara and they get Tizita and Dawait is the troublesome kid.
Hailu loses his wife Selam, and there is a shift in the way life is for the family. Hailu’s
family faces different challenges such as conflicts between Hailu and his troublesome
son Dawait. There is also conflict between Dawait and his brother Yonas.However, amidst
the conflict, there is a sense of protection whereby Hailu and Yonas were ready to protect
Dawait when the soldiers came looking for him. When Hailu is taken to jail, Dawait goes
looking for him even if they are having conflicts. Also, Yonas goes round looking for Dawait
even in morgues even if they are conflicting.

In our current society, conflict in families happens every now and then but love comes in
between and allows people to solve the conflicts and bond together. Additionally, sibling
fighting is also a common thing in the society today but then they are always willing to
offer protection to each other even if there are conflicts between them.

RELIGION

In Beneath the Lion's Gaze religion has been portrayed to play such a pivotal role in the community and society . Hailu and his family members are portrayed as very religious people .This is evidently shown in numerous occasions throughout the book. For instance Sara who is the wife of Yonas and mother to their daughter Tizita is seen as very prayerful when Tizita falls sick . She fasts for days and even goes as far as crawling in church while praying to God to save and spare her daughter from the cruel hands of death.She does the crawling ritual so much that her knees are badly bruised all in the form of prayer believing that ,that will aid in healing her daughter Tizita.
In another case when Hailu gets arrested Yonas spends most of his time in their family prayer room and is also seen reading the Bible .During the instances when he was faced with uncertainty and problems he is seen to retreat to the prayer room. This is something that is common from most of the family members which is why Hailu even had a prayer room built inside the house during the earlier days.
Religion still has a place in today’s society where it acts as a moral compass and ethical framework. Religion often guides people morally, preventing them from making certain decisions due to the fostering of ethics in humanity . It also gives people a sense of meaning and purpose in their daily lives. Religious people tend to feel like they belong to a certain community e.g like a church where they get support from . This is clearly shown when before a religious person makes a life decision they will first have to pass it by their various religious communities . In a society like Kenya people tend to celebrate occasions generally after or while involving the church like how graduation ceremonies are celebrated in churches as a form of thanksgiving.

POLITICS

The book starts of at the regime of the Emperor Haile Silassie and tries to explain how the emperor ruled the country to some extent, Then the book shows the shift from the royal rule to violent control of the military power which was known as the derg, where power was in the hand of those who held the gun which was under the general Gudu who was the face of the Derg. It also talks about the university students which involved Dawit, who was the younger son of Hailu, who was inspired by the revolutionary ideas and the hopes of justice after which they discovered that the Derg wasn’t actually looking for justice but to take revenge, bring a chaos and mass murdering to the people of the country to the point that the people were living in constant fear of speaking up or even grieve the loss of their own family members.

BETRAYAL

It’s one of the main theme in the Beneath the Lion's Gaze which is among the family of Hailu and Ethiopians state .For example,Hailu being a protagonist in the book he faces betrayal from the assistance Amalz who was his nurse,she discloses that Hailu killed a girl using cyanide injection although it was out of mercy to prevent the girl from suffering from the beating she had received from the derg this lead him to be imprisoned.Dawit go against his family to join the protesting army betraying his own family. The resistance group that is the derg betrayed the people of Ethiopia by causing more conflict and bloodshed in the state. Colonel’s daughter is involved in distributing pamphlets which is illegal hence a form of betraying the authority and traditions which was not allowed in the state. Beneath the lion's Gaze is a metaphor which shows the oppressive gaze of the powerful,which betrayed the people and left them in the sense of defensiveness.

Beneath the Lion's Gaze by Maaza Mengiste is a poignant, emotionally charged novel that explores the turbulent years of the Ethiopian Revolution. Through the lives of a family deeply affected by the political unrest, Mengiste paints a vivid picture of the human cost of revolution—betrayal, loyalty, sacrifice, and violence. The narrative is rich with historical detail, offering insight into a nation’s struggle for freedom, as well as the personal, emotional battles fought within families. Mengiste’s exploration of power, corruption, and trauma makes this a powerful and timeless reflection on the nature of political conflict. With its compelling characters and beautifully crafted prose, this novel not only illuminates Ethiopia's past but also resonates with current global struggles against authoritarianism and violence.

books. Her work can be found in the New
